==================================================
                POKER RULES
        (Texas Hold'em - Standard Version)
==================================================

1. OVERVIEW
-----------
Poker is a card game where players bet on who has the
best hand, or who can convince others to fold. This
document covers the most popular variant: Texas Hold'em.

- Played with a standard 52-card deck.
- 2 to 10 players per table.
- Object: Win chips/money by having the best hand at
  showdown, or by making all other players fold.


2. CARD RANKINGS (Hand Values - Highest to Lowest)
---------------------------------------------------
1. Royal Flush       - A, K, Q, J, 10, all same suit
2. Straight Flush    - 5 cards in sequence, same suit
3. Four of a Kind    - 4 cards of the same rank
4. Full House        - 3 of a kind + a pair
5. Flush             - 5 cards of the same suit (any order)
6. Straight          - 5 cards in sequence (mixed suits)
7. Three of a Kind   - 3 cards of the same rank
8. Two Pair          - 2 different pairs
9. One Pair          - 2 cards of the same rank
10. High Card        - No combination; highest card wins

Note: In case of a tie, the kicker (highest remaining
card) decides the winner.


3. CARD RANK ORDER (High to Low)
---------------------------------
A(Ace), K(King), Q(Queen), J(Jack), 10, 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2
(Ace can also count as low, e.g., in A-2-3-4-5 straight)


4. GAME SETUP
-------------
- Each player is given a starting stack of chips.
- A dealer button (marker) rotates clockwise after each hand.
- Two forced bets start the action:
  * Small Blind  - placed by player left of the dealer
  * Big Blind    - placed by player left of small blind
                   (usually double the small blind)


5. GAME FLOW (Betting Rounds)
------------------------------

STEP 1: HOLE CARDS (Pre-Flop)
  - Each player is dealt 2 private cards (hole cards).
  - Betting round begins with the player left of the big
    blind.
  - Options: Fold, Call, Raise.

STEP 2: THE FLOP
  - 3 community cards are dealt face-up in the middle.
  - Betting round begins with the first active player
    left of the dealer.

STEP 3: THE TURN
  - 1 more community card is dealt (4 total on the table).
  - Another betting round occurs.

STEP 4: THE RIVER
  - 1 final community card is dealt (5 total on the table).
  - Final betting round occurs.

STEP 5: SHOWDOWN
  - Remaining players reveal their hole cards.
  - Best 5-card hand (using any combination of the 2 hole
    cards and 5 community cards) wins the pot.
  - If two or more players tie, the pot is split.


6. PLAYER ACTIONS
------------------
- FOLD  : Give up your hand and forfeit any chance at the pot.
- CHECK : Pass the action without betting (only if no bet
          is currently active).
- CALL  : Match the current bet on the table.
- BET   : Be the first to place chips in the pot during a round.
- RAISE : Increase the size of an existing bet.
- ALL-IN: Bet all of your remaining chips.


7. BETTING STRUCTURES
-----------------------
- No-Limit   : Players may bet any amount, up to their
               entire stack, at any time.
- Pot-Limit  : Maximum bet/raise is limited to the current
               size of the pot.
- Fixed-Limit: Bets and raises are fixed to preset amounts.


8. THE POT AND SIDE POTS
--------------------------
- All bets/calls/raises go into a central "pot."
- If a player goes all-in with fewer chips than others,
  a "side pot" is created for the remaining players to
  keep betting among themselves.
- The all-in player can only win the main pot (up to the
  amount they contributed), not the side pot(s).


9. END OF A HAND
------------------
A hand ends when either:
  a) Only one player remains (all others folded) - that
     player wins the pot without showdown, OR
  b) Multiple players reach showdown - best hand wins.

After the hand, the dealer button moves one position
clockwise, and a new hand begins.


10. BASIC STRATEGY TIPS (Optional Reference)
-----------------------------------------------
- Starting hands like A-A, K-K, Q-Q, and A-K are strong
  pre-flop hands.
- Position matters: acting later in a round gives more
  information advantage.
- Bluffing is a valid strategy - betting/raising with a
  weak hand to make opponents fold.
- Pot odds: compare the cost of a call to the size of the
  pot to decide if a call is mathematically worthwhile.


11. COMMON VARIANTS (For Reference)
--------------------------------------
- Texas Hold'em   : 2 hole cards + 5 community cards (most popular)
- Omaha           : 4 hole cards (must use exactly 2) + 5 community cards
- Seven-Card Stud : No community cards; 7 cards dealt per
                    player (3 down, 4 up) across multiple rounds
- Five-Card Draw  : Each player gets 5 private cards and
                    may discard/replace some before showdown
